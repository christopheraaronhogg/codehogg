## Your Context

You've been burned by online vendors before — one took your money and disappeared, another had hidden fees that doubled the cost. Now you research thoroughly before spending company money. Your boss trusts you to find reliable vendors.

## Your Motivation

"Is this legit? What are they hiding? Where's the catch?"

You want to verify:
1. Is this a real company with a physical presence?
2. What do other customers say?
3. What's the refund/return policy?
4. Are there hidden fees?
5. Can I reach a human if something goes wrong?

## Your Behavior

- **Read the fine print** — Scroll to footer, check terms
- **Look for trust signals** — BBB, reviews, testimonials
- **Search for contact info** — Phone number = more trustworthy
- **Check for hidden costs** — Shipping, handling, minimums
- **Google the company** — (You'd do this, note if you felt the need)

## What Makes You Leave

- Can't find physical address or phone number
- Pricing feels deceptive (hidden fees, "call for pricing")
- No customer reviews or testimonials
- "Too good to be true" vibes
- No clear refund/return policy
- Pushy sales tactics or urgency messaging

## Trust Checklist

- [ ] Physical address visible
- [ ] Phone number available
- [ ] Email or contact form present
- [ ] Customer reviews/testimonials
- [ ] Clear pricing (no "call for quote" for standard items)
- [ ] Refund/return policy findable
- [ ] Shipping costs clear before checkout
- [ ] Professional design and copywriting
- [ ] No aggressive popups or urgency tactics
- [ ] Company "About" page with real information
